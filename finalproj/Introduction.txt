Inspired by the applicable nature of the speed-dating problem in homework-4, the team set out to expand its analysis to better answer the question "which attribute(s) matter the most for obtaining a second date" through 3 key ways. 
First, instead of only analyzing the attractiveness, fun, and shared-interests parameters, we would analyze all 6 parameters from the Kaggle dataset. 
Next, this analysis is performed in the lens of constructing a Bayesian Logistic Regression model, which has great compatibility with our data since the 'match' parameter is binary and our system's posterior is simply the product of a Bernoulli likelihood and our prior. 
This aspect of the project is centered around continued application of Stan's Monte Carlo Markov Chain algorithm. 
After model convergence, we then generate new data and compare the most successful parameters using the artificial data to our ground truth to illuminate how robust our model is. 
Lastly, we expand our modeling experimentation via developing a Hierarchical Bayesian Logistic model, compare the results of the two implementations, and prompt an LLM to see if it can generate a model that produces more accurate data than our own. 
The ultimate aim of the project is to synthesize many different facets of cognitive modeling in an intriguing real-world scenario.
